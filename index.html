Our Beginning

From a Small Dream to a Legacy

In 2004, Chef Arjun Kapoor left his position at a five-star hotel with one dream: to open a restaurant that felt like eating at his mother's table — but for the world. He carried with him a battered notebook filled with his grandmother's recipes, handwritten in Hindi.

Spice Heritage opened its doors in Bandra West with just 12 tables and a kitchen powered by three people. Within a year, the queues stretched down the street. Within a decade, it became one of India's most celebrated dining destinations.

Today, we serve over 500 guests every day, but the soul remains the same: authentic, heartfelt Indian food made with the finest ingredients and infinite care.

The Team

Meet Our Chefs

✦

Chef Arjun Kapoor

Founder & Executive Chef

Trained at Le Cordon Bleu and the ITC Grand Central, Arjun brings 25 years of expertise. His speciality is Mughlai cuisine and slow-cooked preparations.

Chef Meera Iyer

Head Chef – South Indian

From a family of cooks in Chettinad, Meera mastered over 200 regional recipes before joining Spice Heritage. She heads our South Indian and seafood menu.

Chef Ranjit Singh

Tandoor & Bread Specialist

A fourth-generation tandoor specialist from Amritsar. His breads — naans, kulchas, and parathas — have earned a devoted following among our regulars.

What We Stand For

Our Mission & Values

✦
🌿

Authenticity

Every recipe traces back to a real family kitchen. We use traditional methods — stone grinding, dum cooking, wood-fired tandoors — that honor the origin of each dish.

♻️

Sustainability

We source 80% of our produce from certified organic farms within 200km. Zero single-use plastic. We compost all kitchen waste and run on renewable energy.

🤝

Community

We train and employ over 40 people from the local community. Our apprenticeship program has launched the careers of more than 100 chefs across India.

🎨

Innovation

While rooted in tradition, we embrace creativity. Our seasonal tasting menus push the boundaries of Indian cuisine while always remaining true to its soul.
